Welcome! I'm Nia Quinn, a second-year Computer Science and Math student at Northeastern University with a passion for optimization and algorithm design. During my time at Northeastern, I've had the opportunity to explore my interests through a variety of activities, including being a Teaching Assistant for an introductory computer science course, being a software co-lead as part of AerospaceNU's Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (NUAV) team, leading the Club Running team as a captain, and even whipping up dumplings with the Cooking Club. These experiences have taught me valuable skills in leadership, project management, and collaboration.

After joining NUAV in January of 2023, the realm of possibility expanded vastly. With the help of experienced upperclassmen, I was introduced to exciting projects and dove headfirst into the software team. Our project? Obstacle avoidance. Using WeBots, robotics simulation software running in a Linux VM, we we communicated with the simulated drone via ArduPilot commands. A camera stream from the drone was accessed via Sockets and was to be used as input to a Proximal Policy Optimization algorithm, that once trained, would allow a drone to be placed in a random environment and avoid obstacles along a path. The repo for this is starred in the link to my GitHub on the left.
